## 1. Scenario 

A phishing email was analysed , in which the threat vector impersonates an attorney from Sullivan & Cromwell 

## 2. Email summary 

The email appeared to be sent from an internal member of Sullivan & Cromwells legal team. It claimed that there was an overdue invoice issued to the receivers company . It included a message directing the user to settle the account urgently. 

## 3. Analysis

### Identified Indicators
1. The sender email address mimics the legitimate Sullivan & Cromwell one, but there is a slight variation in the domain , with "Sullivan" instead being spelt as "sullivam" in the malicious email.
2. The email contains grammatical errors ,  such as the use of the word "advise" instead of "advised" and the absence of a fullstop at the end of the email body.
